My Favorite Automobiles: A Semantic Webpage

Rear Wheel Drive

RWD is the drivetrain god intended.
It makes drives exciting. RWD cars tend to oversteering during a corner. It's easy to kick the tail of car out and drift your way around a corner. RWD is the go to if you want performance driving.

All Wheel Drive

It has so much understeer! AWD cars are great for flooring the gas peddle out of a corner, as it has great traction. But it also tends to understeer during a corner. Now some might say this is great for a daily driver. It's safe and predictable, but I say where is the fun in that. Try nailing the apex of a corner with all that understeer!

Conclusion

In the end, both setup has its pros and cons. Both are safe and reiable options as long as the driver is skilled and understand the different characteristics of each drivetrain.

But, RWD is the preferred option for professional circuit racing, performance driving and enthusiasts. Not a fact, only my opinion.
